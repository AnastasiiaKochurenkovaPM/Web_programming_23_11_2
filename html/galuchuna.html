GALICIA


The historic region in western Ukraine, lying north of the Carpathians and bounded on the east by the river Zbruch. Among the local tourist attractions are Olesky, Pidhoretsky and Svirsky castles, as well as the rock fortress Tustan. The old part of the city of Lviv, which is the cultural center of Ukrainian Galicia, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Modern creative and business centers are being created on the basis of old industrial zones in Galicia.

Zolochiv Castle



Zolochiv Castle is a historical and architectural monument of the 17th century of national importance, revived from oblivion by the incredible efforts of B.G. Voznitsky, who devoted his entire life to preserving the Ukrainian cultural heritage. The reconstructed Zolochiv castle complex impresses with the contrast of lyrical beauty (of which only the flirty Chinese palace is worth) and the drama of events, the memory of which is kept within its silent walls. One of the most interesting exhibits in Zolochiv Castle are stones with encrypted inscriptions. Inscriptions date from the end of XIV - the beginning. XV century, have no analogues, not yet reported. The stones were brought from the outskirts of Zolochev - the village of Novosilky.

Dzhurynsky waterfall



Dzhurynsky waterfall, which is part of the Dniester Canyon National Natural Park, is considered one of the most picturesque and interesting places in the Ternopil region. Getting to the hydrological natural monument is not easy, but that does not stop those who know about it. To get a sea of ​​positive emotions, hide from the scorching sun in summer, have a good time in the company - this is what people come here for even from abroad. Dzhurinsky waterfall has another name - Chervonogorodsky. Dzhurin is the name of the local river, tributaries of the Dniester. But the ancient city of Chervonogorod is no longer on any map of the world.
